Positions

CSIRO: Knowledge Common

Working in the Knowledge Common (Track 2) group, in the Valuing Sustainability Future Science Platform at the CSIRO Australia, I analysed the production of new knowledge in form of green technological innovation generated by the emission trading scheme (EU-ETS). We find that carbon pricing effectively enhances the transition to a low-carbon economy for countries' Net-zero target.

Sussex SSRP: Financial Crises and Environmental Sustainability

Working with the research unit ‘Financial Crises and Environmental Sustainability (FCES)’ of the Sussex Sustainability Research Project (SSRP). This project sheds new light on the interplay between financial crises and sustainable development. This study investigates the environmental impacts of the economic slowdown known as the "Great Stagnation," revealing complex outcomes: biodiversity loss and reduced global fish stocks contrast with improvements in air quality and reduced forest loss. It concludes that economic slowdowns alone do not guarantee sustainable environmental outcomes, emphasizing the need for comprehensive environmental policies during all economic conditions.

APM: Project Management Conditions for Project Success

The research addresses project-based organizations and professionals, outlining strategies for enhancing project success, such as leveraging technology and AI, prioritizing informal mentoring, and establishing roles for knowledge management. Key findings include nine dynamic conditions—ranging from interpersonal skills to sustainability—that are crucial for adapting project management practices to contemporary challenges, including those posed by the Covid-19 pandemic.
